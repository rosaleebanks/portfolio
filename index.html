About Me

Rosalee Banks is a doctoral student in Criminal Justice at Temple University. She completed her BA and MA in Criminal Justice at Temple, where she was awarded the Criminal Justice Excellence Award.

Her research focuses on political extremism, white supremacy in modern contexts, juvenile justice, and the intersections of radicalism and digital media.

She has presented at the American Society of Criminology and is currently working on projects examining divisive propaganda, hate speech, and identity formation.

Research Interests

  • Political extremism and radicalization
  • White supremacy and identity formation
  • Juvenile justice
  • Digital media and propaganda

Current Research

Divisive Propaganda and Identity Formation

This project examines how online hate speech and propaganda contribute to identity formation and social division. Using mixed methods approaches, it explores how narratives are constructed and internalized across digital environments.

Patriotic Masculinity and Authority

This research analyzes how patriotic masculinity is performed to reinforce authority and suppress opposition, drawing on qualitative interviews and symbolic interactionist frameworks.

Professional Activity

Rosalee serves as President of the Criminal Justice Honor Society and has presented research at national conferences, including the American Society of Criminology.
